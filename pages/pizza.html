Pizza

Home

Description

A classic that everyone loves. It doesn't have to be as intimidating as one would think. Anyone can make pizza with only a couple tools that they may not already have, and it's almost certain they won't be disappointed with the results. The purpose of this recipe is to provide a relatively easy way for anyone to make pizza in their home kitchen.

Ingredients

Dough

  • 2 1/4 cups (530 ml) warm water
  • 1 tbsp sugar (12g) sugar
  • 1 tbsp (9g) active dry yeast
  • 2 tbsp (30 ml) olive oil
  • 1 tbsp (18 g) kosher salt
  • 5 cups (600g) bread flour, plus more for working the dough
  • Additional oil for greasing the dough
  • cornmeal, semolina flour, or coarse-ground whole wheat flour for dusting

Sauce

  • 1 28 oz (828 ml) can crushed tomatoes
  • 2-4 tbsp (30-60 ml) olive oil
  • 1 tsp (4g) sugar
  • 2 tsp (a fraction of a gram) dried oregano

Cheese

  • 24-32 oz (680-910 g) whole-milk, low-moisture mozzarella, freshly grated (6-8 oz / 170-225 g per pizza)
  • grated parmesan for dusting (10 g per pizza)

Steps

Dough

  1. Combine the water, sugar and yeast in a large bowl and let sit for a few minutes. If the yeast goes foamy, it's alive and you're good to proceed (if it doesn't, it's dead and you need new yeast).
  2. Add the olive oil and salt and 5 cups (600g) of bread flour. Mix until just combined.
  3. Add just enough additional flour to keep the dough workable (i.e. not too sticky) and kneed until you can stretch some of the dough into a thin sheet without it tearing. (NOTE: You will probably need to add a lot more flour. The quantity I give here is just a base line to get your started.)
  4. Divide the dough into four equal balls and put them in four containers (ideally glass).
  5. Lightly coat the balls and the interior of their containers with olive oil.
  6. Cover, and either rise at room temperature for two hours, or put them in the refrigerator and let them rise for 1-7 days. (I prefer the long, cold rise).
  7. When you want to bake, put a pizza stone or pizza steel into your oven (mine works best on a high rack position but every oven is different).
  8. Preheat to your highest possible temperature, ideally convection, for a full hour.

Sauce

  1. Simply mix together the ingredients for the sauce.

Baking

  1. Liberally dust a pizza peel with cornmeal (or something similar).
  2. Take the cold dough out of the fridge and dust it in flour.
  3. Stretch to the widest size and shape that will fit on your peel and stone/steel.
  4. Top with just enough sauce to lightly coat the surface.
  5. Dust the sauce layer with parmesan, then cover with the mozzarella.
  6. Transfer the pizza to the stone/steel and bake until the crust is well-browned and the cheese has browned a bit (but, ideally, has not started oozing out an orange grease layer), 6-7 minutes.
